# 🎲 Chowka Bhara (Ashta Chamma / Chauka Bara)

**Chowka Bhara** (also known by various regional names like *Ashta Chamma*, *Chauka Bara*, *Daayam*, or *Katla*) is one of the oldest traditional board games from India, still played today. It is a strategic cross-and-circle race game for 2 to 4 players, utilizing cowrie shells as dice.

---

## 1. History & Cultural Significance

* **Ancient Origins:** The game's origins trace back to ancient India. Historically, variations of it have been documented in the Vedic period. It belongs to the same family of traditional Indian race games as *Pachisi* (Ludo's predecessor) and *Kavade*.
* **Epic References:** Ancient texts and epics like the *Mahabharata* contain mentions of board games played with dice and cowrie shells, sharing rules and paths identical to Chowka Bhara.
* **Regional Names:**
  * **Chauka Bara / Katla / Katte Mane / Gatta Mane / Chakaara** – Kannada (Karnataka)
  * **Ashta Chamma** – Telugu (Andhra Pradesh / Telangana)
  * **Daayam / Thaayam / Dayakattai** – Tamil (Tamil Nadu)
  * **Kavidi Kali / Pakidakali** – Malayalam (Kerala)
  * **Changaasta / Atthu / Kanna Kauri / Chunga** – Hindi (North India)
  * **Chomal Ishto / Ahmedabad Baji** – Gujarati (Gujarat)
  * **Champool / Challas Aath** – Marathi (Maharashtra)

---

## 2. Basic Setup & Components

* **The Board:** The board is usually a square grid made of a $5 \times 5$ or $7 \times 7$ matrix.
  * **Crossed Squares (Safe Houses):** Selected cells on the grid (typically the four outer middle cells and the absolute center square) are marked with crosses. These are neutral zones where pawns cannot be captured.
* **Pawns:** Each player starts with 4 pawns (historically tamarind seeds, small stones, or colored shells) placed in their respective yard or starting safe houses.
* **Dice (Cowrie Shells):** Players use 4 cowrie shells (in 5x5 grids) or 6 cowrie shells (in 7x7 grids) to roll.

---

## 3. Rules & Gameplay

### Rolling Mappings (using 4 Cowrie Shells)
The number of cowrie shells landing mouth-up determines the move value:

* **1 Mouth Up:** Move 1 square.
* **2 Mouths Up:** Move 2 squares.
* **3 Mouths Up:** Move 3 squares.
* **4 Mouths Up:** Move 8 squares + Earns an **Extra Roll**.
* **0 Mouths Up:** Move 4 squares + Earns an **Extra Roll**.

*Note: In 7x7 board versions using 6 cowrie shells, 6 mouths up (Chowka) gives 6 steps, and 0 mouths up (Bara) gives 12 steps, both awarding extra rolls.*

---

## 4. The Path & Movement

1. **Outer Ring (Anti-Clockwise):** All players enter the board from their respective starting safe houses and travel anti-clockwise along the outermost loop of the grid.
2. **Inner Ring Entry:** Players cannot enter the inner rings of the board until they have **captured (cut)** at least one opponent's pawn. 
3. **Inner Loop (Clockwise):** Once a player completes a capture, their path moves into the inner concentric loop, moving in a clockwise direction.
4. **Final Home (Ghar):** After navigating the inner loop, pawns enter the center-most square. The first player to get all four pawns to the center wins the game.

---

## 5. Capturing & Safe Zones

* **Capturing (Cutting):** If your pawn lands on a non-safe cell occupied by an opponent's pawn, the opponent's pawn is captured. It is removed from the board and returned to their starting yard. You also earn an **Extra Roll** as a reward.
* **Safe Houses (Crossed Cells):** Safe cells are neutral ground. Pawns of different colors can share safe cells peacefully without any captures occurring.
